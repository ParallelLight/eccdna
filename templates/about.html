Welcome to TeCD

(The eccDNA Collection Database)

As we all know, DNA is located on the chromosome, but in fact, there is DNA outside the chromosome, and there is a special kind of circular DNA, which is to introduce the protagonist eccDNA. Circular DNA is a common form of DNA molecule in nature. For example, the genomic DNA of bacteria or yeast, bacterial plasmid, mitochondrial DNA and so on are all circular DNA molecules. In eukaryotes, there is a special class of circular DNA molecules, which are separated or detached from the normal genome, dissociated from the chromosome genome, and participate in physiological or pathological processes in a special way. Because they are independent DNA molecules outside the chromosome, they are collectively referred to as extrachromosomal DNA, and they are often circular, so they are called extrachromosomal circular DNA, or eccDNA for short. The particles of eccDNA is usually less than 1 kb and invisible by light microscopy. TeCD attempts to collect all existing information about eccDNA. For now, TeCD has been collecting the molecular information of eccDNA for Homo Sapiens and Saccharomyces Cerevisiae. The researchers can search the gene or location associated eccDNA from TeCD, and detect the sequence associated genes by BLAST tool.

About
Here you can find the meaning of the columns and the Samples in the dataset.

Columns About
ID The unique code of each eccDNA. Different organisms have different codes, such as ecc_ H is Homo, and ecc_sc is saccharomyces cerevisiae.
ORGANISM The organism of eccDNA.
SAMPLE The tissue or cell type to which eccDNA belongs.
CHROM The chromosome of eccDNA.
START The start site of eccDNA.
END The end site of eccDNA.
LENGTH The length of eccDNA.
GENE The Genes on eccDNA.(You can learn more about gene by clicking on the link)
SEQUENCE The sequence of eccDNA.
PMID PubMed ID of the datasets source.
